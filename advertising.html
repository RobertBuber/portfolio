Detroit Bikes
Detroit Bikes is a newer company that makes bikes the old-fashioned way. They wanted to communicate thier values of local, handmade, high-quality products to a larger audience in a fun way. They wanted to make ads that people would not only look at, but hang on their wall.
Glad
We needed a campaign to differentiate Glad from the growing number of generic-brand plastic products in a fun way so when shoppers think food, they think Glad. And we wanted the brand to be instantly recognizable so when they think quality, they think Glad.
adidas
Adidas doesn't have consistent messaging across products. We unified it with a series of work that celebrates the people who shape the brand. We wanted every phase of purchase to feel like going further into an adventure.
Gillette
After 100 years, Gillette is seen as a brand for older, more serious people. Add to that: younger (13-30) men see facial hair as a badge of manliness. We wanted to show that grooming feels good, looks good and Gillette easily slices through the monster that is body hair.
Harley-Davidson Clothes
Not a lot of women buy motorcycles. But, many of them have a biker kind of personality. Harley-Davidson isn't for bikers. It's for powerful, assertive, & indepedant people. By focusing on that, instead of just bikes, Harley becomes much more versatile.
Sony
Sony has some of the best-reviewed noise-cancelling headphones. And unlike other brands, that focus on the extremes of fashion or tech, we wanted to showcase noise that we face every day. So we imagined scenarios where the noise has been mysteriously quieted.
